Paneer Butter Masala

Ingredients (Serves 4) ‚¨áÔ∏è

  • 250g paneer (cubed)
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 large onion (finely chopped)
  • 2 large tomatoes
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/4 cup fresh cream
  • 1/4 cup cashew paste (soak cashews and blend with water)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)
  • 1 cup water

Step-by-Step Process ‚¨áÔ∏è

  1. Prep Paneer & Cashew
  2. Cut paneer into cubes and keep aside.
  3. Soak 10-12 cashews in warm water for 15 minutes and grind to a smooth paste.
  4. Heat Butter & Oil
  5. In a pan, heat 2 tbsp oil + 2 tbsp butter on medium flame.
  6. Add cumin seeds; let them splutter.
  7. Saut√© Onions
  8. Add finely chopped onions and saut√© until golden brown.
  9. Add 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste and fry for 1-2 minutes until raw smell disappears.
  10. Add Spices & Tomato Puree
  11. Add turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder; saut√© for 30 seconds.
  12. Add tomato puree and cook for 5-6 minutes until oil separates from masala.
  13. Mix Cashew Paste
  14. Stir in cashew paste with 1 cup water.
  15. Let it simmer for 5 minutes to thicken the gravy.
  16. Add Paneer & Cream
  17. Gently add paneer cubes into the gravy.
  18. Stir in fresh cream.
  19. Sprinkle garam masala and salt to taste.
  20. Simmer for 3-4 minutes.
  21. Garnish & Serve
  22. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  23. Serve hot with naan, roti, or steamed rice.
  24. Tips: For extra richness, use unsalted butter and full-fat cream. You can lightly fry the paneer cubes before adding for a golden crust.



Dal Makhani

Ingredients (Serves 4-5) ‚¨áÔ∏è

  • 1 cup whole Urad Dal (Black Gram)
  • 1/4 cup Rajma (Kidney Beans)
  • 4 cups water (for pressure cooking)
  • 2 tablespoons butter (or ghee)
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1 large onion (finely chopped)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 medium tomatoes (pureed)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon Garam Masala
  • 1/2 cup fresh cream
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)
  • 1 green chili (slit, optional)

Step-by-Step Process ‚¨áÔ∏è

  1. Soak Dal & Rajma
  2. Wash and soak Urad Dal and Rajma overnight (or for at least 8 hours). Drain the water.
  3. Pressure Cook
  4. Transfer the soaked Dal and Rajma to a pressure cooker with 4 cups of fresh water and a little salt.
  5. Cook for 8-10 whistles until the lentils are very soft and mushy.
  6. Prepare Masala
  7. In a separate heavy-bottomed pan, heat **butter** and **oil** on medium heat.
  8. Add the finely chopped onion and saut√© until golden brown.
  9. Add **ginger-garlic paste** and slit green chili (if using); fry for 1 minute until the raw smell is gone.
  10. Add Spices & Tomato Puree
  11. Add **turmeric, red chili, cumin, and coriander powders**. Saut√© for 30 seconds.
  12. Pour in the tomato puree. Cook the masala for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the fat separates.
  13. Combine and Simmer
  14. Add the cooked (and mashed slightly) Dal and Rajma mixture to the masala pan.
  15. Stir well, crushing some of the dal against the side of the pan to thicken the gravy.
  16. Add 1/2 cup to 1 cup of water if the consistency is too thick.
  17. Let it simmer on **low heat** for at least **20-30 minutes**. The longer it simmers, the better the flavor.
  18. Finish & Garnish
  19. Stir in **Garam Masala** and adjust salt.
  20. Turn off the heat and stir in the **fresh cream**. (Keep a little cream aside for garnishing).
  21. Garnish with a swirl of fresh cream and fresh coriander leaves.
  22. Serve hot with plain rice, jeera rice, naan, or tandoori roti.
